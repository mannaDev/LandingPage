Description

Generic drugs are copies of branded drugs, which can only be produced after the branded drug’s patent has expired. Although they are identical to their branded counterparts, they are priced at significant discounts to the branded price. As generic drug makers go through an abbreviated and less costly process to get an approval from the govt. agencies, prices of generics are relatively lower. Currently, on average, the cost of a generic drug is 80 to 85 percent lower than the branded product.

Public and private spending on pharmaceuticals account for a substantial fraction of the total expenses of health care in developed and developing countries (WHO, 2000; OECD, 2011). And optimizing this cost is of importance to control spending. By 2016, it is expected that the value of the total global generics sector will have risen to $358 billion, representing more than 18% of all pharmaceuticals.

The owner of the branded drugs or (old-)originals compete with the generic companies for the same molecule. The generic drugs are purchased by Hospitals through a bidding process wherein several vendors compete for contracts. Vendor offering the lowest price to hospital wins the bid for a certain contractual duration.

Objective

There are 2 main objectives to this contest:

  • 1. Build a model to predict winning bid price for products after LOE (Loss of Exclusivity) for different geographies (identified by account, province, region)
    • 1. Predict Bid Price for products with Molecules 1 and 2 as active ingredients after LOE i.e. in 2016
  • 2. Based on modeling, prepare a report to understand the dynamics of pricing strategies of vendors for different molecules in different markets. Some of the questions the report should be able to answer are as follows:
    • 1. Vendors with aggressive pricing strategies
    • 2. Geographies where pricing is aggressive or less aggressive
    • 3. Segments where vendors tend to have more or less focus
    • 4. How presence of multiple vendors impact bidding prices
    • 5. Impact of volumes on winning bids
    • 6. Trends of bidding prices and its impact on new molecules.

Data

Bidding price data is provided for 4 molecules across different accounts and regions along with winner and competitor’s bids. Each data record consists of the following attributes.

  • a. Row and Account ID (Account is indicative of hospital purchasing the drug)
  • b. Geographic information – region, province
  • c. Bid Info – Length, Volume, Dates: Offer, Contract start, Contract end
  • d. Molecule Type, Presentation
  • e. Winning Bid, Product, Company
  • f. Competitors Bidding price & company

Contest Tasks

The contest has two mandatory tasks:

  • Task 2: Model/Report Submission Task (ONLY Conditional Winners)
